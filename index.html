Biohazard:

Founded in 1866, THE Ill HARMONIC was described by The Daily Illini as “[The Ill Harmonic is]...good...music...yes.” Founded by Ret. Col. James A. Q. P. W. Buckland, The Ill Harmonic specializes in a unique blend of alpha-male and beta-male voices. Long-time friend and mentor of Buckland’s, Charlie Boardman, a freshman at University of Wisconsin, has been a member of the group for just over 2 weeks; describes the group as “A fun experience for all. Please join.”

THE Ill HARMONIC has received a variety of awards, honors, and recognition. For example, one example is that of the “Best Up And Coming Male Acapella Group at UIUC Award,” and others.

The Ill HARMONIC performs at weddings, bar/bat mitzvahs, bar/bat fights, and school events such as the annual UIUC Sing-a-thon to raise money for special-needs students. Many people were in attendance. They raised a lot of money for the cause. It was very much fun!!:) Please listen to our music and check out our songs on the songs page by clicking here.
